Continual Learning

Brief Description

Continual learning empowers machines to approach human-level intelligence, continuously and quickly learning new tasks with a small amount of labeled data and without forgetting previously learned knowledge. In this tutorial, we~provide a review of the current literature in this field, with a specific on continual learning of large-scale pre-trained models.


Overview

Human intelligence excels at continuously accumulating knowledge and quickly adapting to novel tasks over time, though deep learning still struggles to either retain previously learned knowledge or transfer them for fast adaptation as new tasks arrive. This gap has spawned the burgeoning field of continual learning, which aims to enable deep neural networks to accumulate knowledge incrementally in a non-stationary environment without forgetting and with forward transfer. In light of the ongoing paradigm shift in artificial intelligence towards pre-training and fine-tuning, the continual update of gigantic pre-trained models presents both unprecedented challenges and great opportunities for the field.


This tutorial begins by providing an overview of the problem definition, expected capabilities and evaluation metrics in continual learning. We then review the current literature on conventional continual learning, covering the following three topics:

  • Existing settings and benchmarks, to assess their relevance to real-world continual learning applications
  • Trending techniques for avoiding catastrophic forgetting and their effectiveness.
  • Trending techniques for promoting forward transfer and their effectiveness.


Next, we review extended continual learning approaches for large-scale pre-trained models, by discussing (1) new challenges and opportunities presented, and (2) trending techniques. Finally, we conclude with a discussion of successful applications and open questions for future research in continual learning.
